Nomenclature & Basics Atomic
Q1Multiple Choice

Which of the following correctly names the ionic compound FeCl₃?

  • A Iron chloride
  • B Iron(III) chloride
  • C Triiron chloride
  • D Iron(III) trichloride
✅ B — Iron(III) chloride. Iron is a transition metal with multiple possible charges, so a Roman numeral is required. The 3 Cl⁻ ions (each −1) balance the Fe³⁺, confirming the +3 charge. Never use Greek prefixes (like tri-) in ionic compound names.
Q2Multiple Choice

What is the correct IUPAC name for N₂O₄?

  • A Nitrogen oxide
  • B Dinitrogen oxide
  • C Dinitrogen tetroxide
  • D Dinitrogen tetraoxide
✅ C — Dinitrogen tetroxide. N₂O₄ is a covalent compound (two nonmetals), so Greek prefixes apply: di- for 2 nitrogen atoms, tetr- for 4 oxygen atoms. Drop the extra 'a' before 'oxide' → tetroxide, not tetraoxide.
Q3Multiple Choice

HBr dissolved in water is correctly named:

  • A Hydrobromic acid
  • B Bromic acid
  • C Bromous acid
  • D Hydrogen bromide acid
✅ A — Hydrobromic acid. HBr is a binary acid (H + one nonmetal, no oxygen). Binary acids follow the pattern: Hydro- + root + -ic + Acid. The root of bromine is "brom", giving hydrobrom-ic acid.
Q4Multiple Choice

Which compound below contains a polyatomic anion?

  • A NaCl
  • B MgO
  • C CaF₂
  • D Ca(NO₃)₂
✅ D — Ca(NO₃)₂. The nitrate ion (NO₃⁻) is a polyatomic anion — it contains more than one atom bonded together with an overall negative charge. The other options all have monatomic anions (Cl⁻, O²⁻, F⁻).
Q5Multiple Choice

An atom of sulfur has 16 protons and 16 electrons. When it becomes a sulfide ion (S²⁻), it has:

  • A 14 electrons and 16 protons
  • B 18 electrons and 16 protons
  • C 16 electrons and 18 protons
  • D 14 electrons and 14 protons
✅ B — 18 electrons and 16 protons. A 2− charge means the atom gained 2 electrons. 16 + 2 = 18 electrons. The number of protons never changes in ordinary chemistry — the nucleus stays the same.

Stoichiometry Stoich
Q6Multiple Choice

When balancing a chemical equation, which of the following may you change?

  • A Subscripts within a formula
  • B The identity of the reactants
  • C Coefficients in front of formulas
  • D The arrow direction
✅ C — Coefficients. Coefficients are the large numbers in front of each formula. Changing subscripts would create a different substance entirely. The Law of Conservation of Mass requires the same number of each atom on both sides — coefficients achieve this without altering any compound's identity.
Q7Multiple Choice

In the reaction: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu, what type of reaction is this?

  • A Synthesis
  • B Single displacement
  • C Double displacement
  • D Decomposition
✅ B — Single displacement. Zinc (a free element) replaces copper in the compound CuSO₄. The pattern A + BC → AC + B is the hallmark of single displacement. This works because zinc is more reactive than copper.
Q8Computation

How many moles are in 54 g of water (H₂O)? [Molar mass of H₂O ≈ 18 g/mol]

  • A 18 mol
  • B 1 mol
  • C 972 mol
  • D 3 mol
✅ D — 3 mol. moles = mass ÷ molar mass = 54 g ÷ 18 g/mol = 3 mol. Always divide mass by molar mass to convert grams → moles.
Q9Computation

A compound is 40.0% C, 6.7% H, and 53.3% O by mass. What is its empirical formula? [C = 12, H = 1, O = 16]

  • A CH₂O
  • B C₂H₄O₂
  • C CHO
  • D C₂H₂O
✅ A — CH₂O. Assume 100 g: C = 40/12 = 3.33 mol, H = 6.7/1 = 6.7 mol, O = 53.3/16 = 3.33 mol. Divide by smallest (3.33): C = 1, H ≈ 2, O = 1. Empirical formula: CH₂O. Note: CH₂O is also the empirical formula of glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) and acetic acid (C₂H₄O₂) — the molecular formula requires knowing the actual molar mass.
Q10Computation

In the reaction N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃, if 2 mol of N₂ and 3 mol of H₂ are mixed, which is the limiting reagent?

  • A N₂, because it has fewer moles
  • B N₂, because nitrogen is heavier
  • C H₂, because 3 mol H₂ ÷ 3 = 1, while 2 mol N₂ ÷ 1 = 2
  • D Neither — they are present in the exact stoichiometric ratio
✅ C — H₂ is the limiting reagent. Divide moles by stoichiometric coefficient: N₂ → 2/1 = 2; H₂ → 3/3 = 1. The smaller value identifies the limiting reagent. H₂ runs out first, limiting how much NH₃ can form.
Q11Computation

A reaction has a theoretical yield of 25.0 g and an actual yield of 19.5 g. What is the percent yield?

  • A 28.2%
  • B 78.0%
  • C 128%
  • D 44.5%
✅ B — 78.0%. % yield = (actual ÷ theoretical) × 100 = (19.5 ÷ 25.0) × 100 = 78.0%. A yield near 80% is considered good in a lab setting.

Quantum Mechanics & Atomic Structure Quantum
Q12Multiple Choice

Rutherford's gold foil experiment led to which conclusion?

  • A Atoms are indivisible solid spheres
  • B Electrons are embedded throughout a diffuse positive sphere
  • C Atoms have a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus surrounded by mostly empty space
  • D Electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed circular paths
✅ C. Most alpha particles passed straight through the gold foil (confirming mostly empty space), but a small fraction bounced back sharply — indicating a concentrated positive charge at the center. This disproved Thomson's plum pudding model.
Q13Multiple Choice

The photoelectric effect demonstrated that light:

  • A Behaves as discrete packets of energy (photons)
  • B Always travels as a continuous wave
  • C Can only eject electrons if the beam is intense enough
  • D Has no relationship to frequency
✅ A. Below a threshold frequency, no electrons are ejected — regardless of intensity. This proved that energy comes in fixed packets (photons). Only a photon with enough individual energy (E = hν) can eject an electron.
Q14Multiple Choice

Which set of quantum numbers is NOT allowed?

  • A n=2, l=1, mₗ=0, mₛ=+½
  • B n=3, l=2, mₗ=−2, mₛ=−½
  • C n=1, l=0, mₗ=0, mₛ=+½
  • D n=2, l=2, mₗ=1, mₛ=+½
✅ D — Not allowed. The rule is l can only range from 0 to n−1. For n=2, l can be 0 or 1 — but not 2. Having l=2 with n=2 violates the quantum number rules.
Q15Multiple Choice

Which of the following is the correct electron configuration for chromium (Cr, Z=24)?

  • A [Ar] 3d⁴ 4s²
  • B [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹
  • C [Ar] 3d⁶ 4s⁰
  • D [Ar] 4s² 4p⁴
✅ B — [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹. Chromium is an anomalous configuration. A half-filled d subshell (3d⁵) is particularly stable, so one electron migrates from 4s² to 3d, giving 3d⁵ 4s¹ instead of the expected 3d⁴ 4s².
Q16Multiple Choice

According to the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle, which pair of properties cannot be simultaneously known with precision?

  • A Mass and charge
  • B Energy and charge
  • C Position and momentum
  • D Spin and mass
✅ C — Position and momentum. Δx · Δp ≥ h/4π. The more precisely you measure where a particle is, the less precisely you can know how fast it's moving — and vice versa. This is not a measurement limitation; it's a fundamental property of quantum systems.
Q17Multiple Choice

The shape of a p orbital (l = 1) is best described as:

  • A Dumbbell-shaped, oriented along an axis
  • B Perfectly spherical
  • C Cloverleaf-shaped
  • D Donut-shaped with a node at the center
✅ A — Dumbbell-shaped. l=0 is spherical (s orbital). l=1 is dumbbell-shaped (p orbital), with three orientations (px, py, pz). l=2 is the cloverleaf (d orbital). The angular momentum quantum number l determines orbital shape.

Periodic Trends Periodic
Q18Multiple Choice

Which of the following correctly describes the trend in atomic radius across Period 3 (Na → Cl)?

  • A Increases, because more electrons are added
  • B Stays the same, because all atoms are in the same period
  • C Increases, because nuclear charge decreases
  • D Decreases, because increasing nuclear charge pulls electrons closer
✅ D. Across a period, protons are added to the nucleus, increasing the positive charge. This stronger pull draws the electron cloud inward, shrinking atomic radius despite the addition of electrons to the same shell.
Q19Multiple Choice

Why does phosphorus (P) have a higher first ionization energy than sulfur (S), despite S being further right on the periodic table?

  • A Phosphorus has more protons
  • B Phosphorus has a half-filled 3p³ subshell, which is extra stable
  • C Sulfur's electrons are more shielded
  • D Phosphorus is a larger atom
✅ B. P has an exactly half-filled 3p³ subshell — one electron per orbital, no forced pairing. This configuration is extra stable. Sulfur's 3p⁴ forces two electrons to share an orbital; that paired electron experiences extra repulsion and is easier to remove, lowering S's IE below P's.
Q20Multiple Choice

In an isoelectronic series — Na⁺, Mg²⁺, and Al³⁺ — all have 10 electrons. Which ion is the smallest?

  • A Na⁺ (11 protons)
  • B Mg²⁺ (12 protons)
  • C Al³⁺ (13 protons)
  • D They are all the same size
✅ C — Al³⁺. When ions are isoelectronic (same electron count), the one with the most protons has the greatest nuclear charge pulling the same 10 electrons inward. More protons = smaller radius. Al³⁺ has 13 protons vs. 12 for Mg²⁺ and 11 for Na⁺.
Q21Multiple Choice

Which element has the highest electronegativity on the Pauling scale?

  • A Fluorine (F)
  • B Oxygen (O)
  • C Nitrogen (N)
  • D Chlorine (Cl)
✅ A — Fluorine. Fluorine has an electronegativity of 4.0 on the Pauling scale — the highest of any element. The order to remember: F (4.0) > O (3.5) > N (3.0) > Cl (3.2). Cl slots between O and N but is often listed after N in mnemonics. These are the top electronegative elements and the driving force behind polar bonds and hydrogen bonding.

Molecular Geometry & Bonding Molecular
Q22Multiple Choice

Water (H₂O) has 4 electron domains around oxygen. What is its molecular geometry?

  • A Tetrahedral
  • B Bent
  • C Linear
  • D Trigonal planar
✅ B — Bent. Oxygen has 4 electron domains (2 bonding pairs to H, 2 lone pairs). The electron geometry is tetrahedral, but molecular geometry only describes where the atoms are — and the 2 lone pairs are invisible in the shape name. With 2 bonds and 2 lone pairs: bent, with a bond angle compressed below 109.5° to about 104.5°.
Q23Multiple Choice

In Valence Bond Theory, a double bond consists of:

  • A Two sigma (σ) bonds
  • B Two pi (π) bonds
  • C One pi (π) bond only
  • D One sigma (σ) bond and one pi (π) bond
✅ D — 1σ + 1π. A single bond is always one σ bond (head-on orbital overlap). A double bond adds one π bond on top (side-by-side p orbital overlap). A triple bond = 1σ + 2π. The first bond between any two atoms is always sigma; additional bonds are always pi.
Q24Multiple Choice

Carbon in methane (CH₄) is sp³ hybridized. What bond angle does this produce?

  • A 109.5°
  • B 120°
  • C 180°
  • D 90°
✅ A — 109.5°. sp³ hybridization = 4 electron domains pointing to the corners of a tetrahedron. This gives bond angles of 109.5°. sp² = 3 domains, 120° (trigonal planar). sp = 2 domains, 180° (linear). The number in the hybridization label tells you how many domains.
Q25Multiple Choice

Molecular Orbital Theory predicts that O₂ is paramagnetic. This means O₂:

  • A Has no unpaired electrons and is repelled by magnets
  • B Has a bond order of 1
  • C Has two unpaired electrons in degenerate π* antibonding orbitals
  • D Cannot form any bonds
✅ C. When MOT fills O₂'s molecular orbitals, two electrons end up in degenerate (equal-energy) π* antibonding orbitals — one in each, by Hund's Rule. Unpaired electrons = paramagnetism (attracted to magnetic fields). This is something Lewis structures and VBT completely failed to predict, making it one of MOT's greatest early successes.
