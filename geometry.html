Reasoning & Logic Q1
Q1Multiple Choice

Which type of reasoning draws a general conclusion from specific examples?

  • A Deductive reasoning
  • B Inductive reasoning
  • C Algebraic reasoning
  • D Conditional reasoning
✅ B — Inductive reasoning. Inductive reasoning observes specific cases and draws a general conclusion — e.g. "1, 3, 5, 7 are odd; therefore all numbers in this pattern are odd." Deductive reasoning works the opposite way: it starts from a general rule and applies it to a specific case. Note: inductive conclusions are probable, not guaranteed — a single counterexample can disprove them.
Q2Multiple Choice

What is the contrapositive of the statement "If it rains, then the ground is wet"?

  • A If the ground is wet, then it rains.
  • B If it does not rain, then the ground is not wet.
  • C If the ground is not wet, then it does not rain.
  • D If it rains and the ground is wet, then it is true.
✅ C. The contrapositive of "If P then Q" is "If not Q then not P." It is always logically equivalent to the original statement. The converse (A) switches P and Q; the inverse (B) negates both P and Q. Only the contrapositive is guaranteed to have the same truth value as the original.
Q3Multiple Choice

A biconditional statement "P if and only if Q" is true when…

  • A P is true and Q is false
  • B P is false and Q is true
  • C P and Q are both false — always
  • D Both P and Q have the same truth value
✅ D. A biconditional (P ↔ Q) is true when both P and Q are true, or when both are false. It combines the conditional (P→Q) and its converse (Q→P). It is false when P and Q have different truth values. Biconditionals are used to write precise definitions in geometry.
Q4Multiple Choice

In a truth table for "P AND Q," how many rows result in TRUE?

  • A 1 — only when both P and Q are true
  • B 2 — when at least one is true
  • C 3 — when at least one is true
  • D 4 — always true
✅ A. Conjunction (P ∧ Q) is true ONLY when both P and Q are true. With 2 variables there are 4 possible rows (TT, TF, FT, FF) — only TT gives TRUE. Compare: disjunction (P ∨ Q, "OR") is true in 3 rows — everything except FF.

Lines, Planes & Angles Q1
Q5Multiple Choice

Two angles are supplementary. One measures 73°. What is the measure of the other?

  • A 17°
  • B 73°
  • C 107°
  • D 127°
✅ C — 107°. Supplementary angles sum to 180°. So 180° − 73° = 107°. Remember: supplementary = 180° (S for Straight line); complementary = 90° (C for Corner). Vertical angles are equal; adjacent angles on a straight line are supplementary.
Q6Multiple Choice

A transversal crosses two parallel lines. Alternate interior angles are…

  • A Supplementary (sum to 180°)
  • B Congruent (equal)
  • C Complementary (sum to 90°)
  • D Adjacent
✅ B — Congruent. When parallel lines are cut by a transversal: alternate interior angles are equal, alternate exterior angles are equal, and corresponding angles are equal. Co-interior angles (same-side interior / consecutive interior) are supplementary (sum to 180°). These relationships can also be used in reverse to prove two lines are parallel.
Q7Multiple Choice

Line m is perpendicular to line n, and line n is perpendicular to line p. What is the relationship between lines m and p?

  • A They are perpendicular
  • B They intersect at 45°
  • C They are skew
  • D They are parallel
✅ D — Parallel. If m ⊥ n and n ⊥ p, then m ∥ p. Both m and p are perpendicular to the same line n, so they must be parallel to each other. This is a key theorem in parallelism and perpendicularity: "Two lines perpendicular to the same line are parallel to each other."

Triangles Q1
Q8Multiple Choice

Two angles of a triangle measure 48° and 67°. What is the measure of the third angle?

  • A 65°
  • B 75°
  • C 115°
  • D 245°
✅ A — 65°. The Triangle Angle Sum Theorem states the interior angles of any triangle always sum to 180°. So the third angle = 180° − 48° − 67° = 65°. This works for all triangles — scalene, isosceles, equilateral, right, obtuse.
Q9Multiple Choice

An exterior angle of a triangle measures 120°. One of the remote interior angles is 45°. What is the other remote interior angle?

  • A 15°
  • B 60°
  • C 75°
  • D 165°
✅ C — 75°. Exterior Angle Theorem: an exterior angle equals the sum of the two remote interior angles. So 120° = 45° + x → x = 75°. You can verify: the third interior angle = 180° − 120° = 60°, and 45° + 75° + 60° = 180° ✓

Cevians, Congruence & Pythagorean Q2
Q10Multiple Choice

The centroid divides each median in what ratio (from vertex to midpoint)?

  • A 1:1
  • B 2:1
  • C 3:1
  • D 1:3
✅ B — 2:1. The centroid (intersection of medians) divides each median in a 2:1 ratio from the vertex. So the distance from vertex to centroid is twice the distance from centroid to midpoint. The centroid is also the center of gravity (balancing point) of the triangle. The four concurrency points: Centroid (medians), Circumcenter (⊥ bisectors), Incenter (angle bisectors), Orthocenter (altitudes).
Q11Multiple Choice

Which congruence postulate requires knowing two sides and the included angle of each triangle?

  • A SSS
  • B ASA
  • C AAS
  • D SAS
✅ D — SAS (Side-Angle-Side). The angle must be included between the two known sides. The five congruence postulates: SSS (3 sides), SAS (2 sides + included angle), ASA (2 angles + included side), AAS (2 angles + non-included side), HyL (hypotenuse + leg, right triangles only). SSA is NOT a valid postulate — it can produce two different triangles.
Q12Multiple Choice

In a right triangle, the two legs measure 5 and 12. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

  • A 13
  • B 17
  • C 60
  • D 169
✅ A — 13. By the Pythagorean Theorem: c² = a² + b² = 5² + 12² = 25 + 144 = 169, so c = √169 = 13. This is the famous 5-12-13 Pythagorean triple. Other common triples: 3-4-5, 8-15-17, 7-24-25. Multiples also work: 6-8-10, 10-24-26.
Q13Multiple Choice

In △ABC, AB = AC. Which of the following must be true?

  • A ∠A = ∠B
  • B ∠A = ∠C
  • C ∠B = ∠C
  • D All angles are equal
✅ C — ∠B = ∠C. The Isosceles Triangle Theorem states: if two sides of a triangle are congruent, the angles opposite those sides are congruent. AB = AC means B and C are the base vertices — so the base angles ∠B and ∠C are equal. The converse is also true: if two angles of a triangle are equal, the sides opposite them are equal.

Similarity & Proportionality Q3
Q14Multiple Choice

Which similarity theorem requires only two pairs of congruent angles?

  • A SSS∼
  • B AA∼
  • C SAS∼
  • D HL∼
✅ B — AA∼ (Angle-Angle Similarity). If two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another, the triangles are similar — because the third angle is automatically determined (all angles sum to 180°). AA is the most commonly used similarity theorem. SAS∼ requires two proportional sides with the included angle equal; SSS∼ requires all three sides proportional.
Q15Multiple Choice

In △ABC, DE ∥ BC where D is on AB and E is on AC. If AD = 4, DB = 6, and AE = 5, what is EC by the Basic Proportionality Theorem?

  • A 5.5
  • B 6
  • C 7.5
  • D 8
✅ C — 7.5. The Basic Proportionality Theorem (Triangle Proportionality Theorem): if a line is parallel to one side of a triangle and intersects the other two sides, it divides them proportionally. So AD/DB = AE/EC → 4/6 = 5/EC → EC = 5 × 6/4 = 30/4 = 7.5.
Q16Multiple Choice

In a parallelogram, opposite sides are always…

  • A Congruent and parallel
  • B Perpendicular
  • C Supplementary
  • D Equal and perpendicular
✅ A — Congruent and parallel. Properties of a parallelogram: (1) opposite sides are congruent and parallel, (2) opposite angles are congruent, (3) consecutive angles are supplementary, (4) diagonals bisect each other. Special parallelograms: a Rectangle adds right angles; a Rhombus adds all sides equal and perpendicular diagonals; a Square has all properties of both.

Circles Q4
Q17Multiple Choice

An inscribed angle intercepts an arc of 140°. What is the measure of the inscribed angle?

  • A 140°
  • B 280°
  • C 40°
  • D 70°
✅ D — 70°. The Inscribed Angle Theorem: an inscribed angle is half the measure of its intercepted arc. So 140° ÷ 2 = 70°. In contrast, a central angle equals its intercepted arc directly. Corollary: all inscribed angles intercepting the same arc are equal; an inscribed angle in a semicircle (intercepting a diameter) is always 90°.
Q18Multiple Choice

A circle has a radius of 7 cm. What is its circumference? Give your answer in exact form.

  • A 7π cm
  • B 49π cm
  • C 14π cm
  • D 28π cm
✅ C — 14π cm. Circumference = 2πr = 2π × 7 = 14π cm. Note: Area = πr² = 49π cm² (choice B) — don't mix up circumference (perimeter, 1D) and area (2D). Choice A (7π) would be πr, and choice D (28π) would be 4πr — neither is a standard formula.
Q19Multiple Choice

A sector has a central angle of 90° in a circle of radius 6 cm. What is the arc length? Give your answer in exact form.

  • A 6π cm
  • B 3π cm
  • C 9π cm
  • D 12π cm
✅ B — 3π cm. Arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr = (90/360) × 2π × 6 = (1/4) × 12π = 3π cm. Note: the sector area = (θ/360°) × πr² = (1/4) × 36π = 9π cm² (choice C — don't confuse arc length with sector area). Choice D (12π) is the full circumference 2πr, and choice A (6π) is half the circumference.

Solids & Trigonometry Q4
Q20Multiple Choice

A rectangular prism has length 5 cm, width 3 cm, and height 4 cm. What is its volume?

  • A 60 cm³
  • B 47 cm³
  • C 94 cm³
  • D 120 cm³
✅ A — 60 cm³. Volume of a prism = Base Area × Height = (l × w) × h = (5 × 3) × 4 = 60 cm³. Surface area = 2(lw + lh + wh) = 2(15 + 20 + 12) = 2 × 47 = 94 cm² (choice C is the surface area). For a cylinder: V = πr²h; SA = 2πr² + 2πrh.
Q21Multiple Choice

In a right triangle, the side opposite to the reference angle θ is 8 and the hypotenuse is 17. What is sin θ?

  • A 17/8
  • B 8/17
  • C 15/17
  • D 8/15
✅ B — 8/17. SOH-CAH-TOA: Sin = Opposite/Hypotenuse = 8/17. The adjacent side = √(17² − 8²) = √(289 − 64) = √225 = 15. So cos θ = 15/17 (C would be this) and tan θ = 8/15. Note: this is the 8-15-17 Pythagorean triple.
Q22Multiple Choice

You are standing 30 m from a building. The angle of elevation to the top is 45°. How tall is the building?

  • A 15 m
  • B 21.2 m
  • C 42.4 m
  • D 30 m
✅ D — 30 m. Using tan θ = opposite/adjacent: tan 45° = height/30. Since tan 45° = 1, height = 30 × 1 = 30 m. At 45° the height equals the horizontal distance — a useful special case. Angle of elevation is measured upward from the horizontal; angle of depression is measured downward. Always draw a diagram and identify opposite, adjacent, hypotenuse relative to θ.
