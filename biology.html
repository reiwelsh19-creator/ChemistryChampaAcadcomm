Foundations of Biology Nature
Q1Multiple Choice

Which of the following is NOT a recognized branch of biology?

  • A Zoology
  • B Botany
  • C Geology
  • D Microbiology
✅ C — Geology. Geology is the study of Earth's physical structure — a branch of Earth Science, not Biology. Zoology (animals), Botany (plants), and Microbiology (microscopic organisms) are all branches of Biology. Biology itself branches by type of organism, biological process, level of organization, and application.
Q2Multiple Choice

Which of the 8 characteristics of life describes an organism maintaining a stable internal environment?

  • A Heredity
  • B Regulation and Homeostasis
  • C Growth and Development
  • D Energy Processing
✅ B — Regulation and Homeostasis. Homeostasis is the process of keeping the internal environment stable. Regulation is how it's achieved — e.g. sweating to cool down, or a single-celled organism using vacuoles to balance internal salt and water. All 8 characteristics: Order/Organization, Heredity, Evolutionary Adaptation, Energy Processing, Response to Stimuli, Reproduction, Regulation & Homeostasis, Growth & Development.
Q3Multiple Choice

Viruses are a tricky edge case for the definition of life. Why?

  • A They can reproduce but only inside a host cell — they can't on their own
  • B They have cells but no DNA
  • C They are made of cells but cannot reproduce
  • D They respond to stimuli but have no metabolism
✅ A. Viruses have some characteristics of life (they can reproduce, they carry genetic information) but they cannot reproduce on their own — only inside a host cell. They also have no cells, no metabolism, and no homeostasis. This is why biologists debate whether viruses are truly "alive."
Q4Multiple Choice

Which unifying theme of biology states that energy flows in one direction and cannot be recycled, while matter can be cycled?

  • A Information
  • B Organization
  • C Evolution
  • D Energy and Matter
✅ D — Energy and Matter. Energy moves in one direction only and is lost as heat at every level (it cannot be recycled). Matter, however, is cycled — due to the conservation of matter, it can only be converted into new forms. The five unifying themes from your reviewer are: Organization, Information, Energy and Matter, Interaction, and Evolution.
Q5Multiple Choice

In the Information unifying theme, DNA cannot leave the nucleus. What does it get transcribed into to carry information to the cytoplasm?

  • A ATP
  • B Protein
  • C mRNA
  • D tRNA
✅ C — mRNA. The flow of information is: DNA → (transcription) → mRNA → (translation) → Proteins. DNA is transcribed into mRNA, which leaves the nucleus and carries the code to ribosomes in the cytoplasm where proteins are built. In transcription: A→U, G→C, C→G, T→A.

Taxonomy & Classification Evolution
Q6Multiple Choice

Who is known as the Father of Modern Taxonomy and created binomial nomenclature?

  • A Aristotle
  • B Carolus Linnaeus
  • C Ernst Haeckel
  • D Carl Woese
✅ B — Carolus Linnaeus (1707–1778). Linnaeus created binomial nomenclature (Genus species), introduced the hierarchical ranks (Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species — remember: King Philip Came Over For Good Soup), and wrote Systema Naturae classifying thousands of species. Aristotle came first but his system was simpler; Haeckel proposed 3 kingdoms; Woese revolutionized taxonomy with rRNA genetics.
Q7Multiple Choice

Carl Woese's Three-Domain System divided life into Bacteria, Archaea, and Eukarya. What revolutionary method did Woese use to propose this system?

  • A Comparing physical body structures
  • B Fossil record analysis
  • C Microscope observations of cell shape
  • D rRNA (ribosomal RNA) genetics
✅ D — rRNA genetics. Woese compared ribosomal RNA sequences across organisms to determine evolutionary relationships at the molecular level. This revealed that Bacteria and Archaea — although both prokaryotes — are as different from each other as either is from eukaryotes, leading to the Three-Domain System replacing Whittaker's 5-kingdom system.
Q8Multiple Choice

What is the correct order of Linnaean classification from broadest to most specific?

  • A Domain → Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species
  • B Species → Genus → Family → Order → Class → Phylum → Kingdom → Domain
  • C Kingdom → Domain → Class → Phylum → Order → Family → Genus → Species
  • D Domain → Species → Genus → Family → Order → Class → Phylum → Kingdom
✅ A. Broadest to most specific: Domain → Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species. Mnemonic: King Philip Came Over For Good Soup (Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species). Species is the most specific level; Domain is the broadest.
Q9Multiple Choice

Which of the following is a common misconception about phylogenetic trees?

  • A Branch points represent common ancestors
  • B Chimps and humans are sister taxa sharing a common ancestor
  • C Species at the top of a tree are "more evolved" than those at the bottom
  • D Phylogenetic trees show descent with modification
✅ C — This is a misconception. Position on a phylogenetic tree does not indicate how "advanced" or "evolved" an organism is. All organisms alive today are equally evolved — they have all survived to the present. Other misconceptions: "modern species evolved from other modern species" and "species closest on the page are closest relatives."

Evolution Evolution
Q10Multiple Choice

Darwin's two main contributions to evolutionary theory are:

  • A Genetics and taxonomy
  • B Natural selection (mechanism) and descent with modification (outcome)
  • C Phylogeny and binomial nomenclature
  • D Inheritance of acquired traits and the ladder of life
✅ B. Darwin's two contributions: (1) Natural selection — the mechanism, individuals with favorable traits survive and reproduce more. (2) Descent with modification — the outcome, species accumulate differences from their ancestors over generations. He developed these ideas from his voyage on the HMS Beagle (1831–1836), especially observations on the Galápagos Islands.
Q11Multiple Choice

Which was a prevailing idea during Darwin's time that he directly challenged?

  • A Species share common ancestors
  • B The Earth is billions of years old
  • C Species are fixed and do not change over time
  • D Natural selection drives evolution
✅ C — Species are fixed. The prevailing idea was that species were created as they are and don't evolve or go extinct. Darwin challenged this directly. Other prevailing ideas he countered: that life was specially created, that the Earth was only a few thousand years old, and that humans are separate from animals.
Q12Multiple Choice

Natural selection has 4 main principles. Which principle states that better traits lead to surviving and reproducing more?

  • A Survival and Reproduction
  • B Variation
  • C Adaptation
  • D Competition
✅ A — Survival and Reproduction. The four principles of natural selection are: (1) Variation — individuals have different traits, (2) Competition — organisms compete for resources, (3) Survival and Reproduction — better traits = survive and reproduce more, (4) Adaptation — beneficial traits become more common over time.
Q13Multiple Choice

What is the key difference between Lamarck's and Darwin's ideas about how evolution works?

  • A Lamarck believed in evolution; Darwin did not
  • B Darwin believed traits acquired during life are inherited; Lamarck did not
  • C Both had identical ideas about how traits are passed on
  • D Lamarck believed changes during an organism's lifetime are inherited; Darwin focused on heritable variation already present in a population
✅ D. Lamarck's key ideas: use and disuse (body parts change when used/not used) and inheritance of acquired characteristics (changes during life are passed on). Darwin's key ideas: evolution acts on heritable variation already present in a population — traits acquired during life are NOT passed on. Both agreed species change over time and that organisms can become better adapted, but disagreed fundamentally on the mechanism.
Q14Multiple Choice

Which type of natural selection favors the average trait and selects against extremes, narrowing the distribution?

  • A Directional selection
  • B Stabilizing selection
  • C Disruptive selection
  • D Artificial selection
✅ B — Stabilizing selection. The three patterns: (1) Stabilizing — the average is favored, extremes are selected against, narrows distribution. (2) Directional — one extreme is favored, shifts distribution left or right. (3) Disruptive — both extremes are favored, the middle does poorly, creates a bimodal distribution.
Q15Multiple Choice

Mutation and sexual reproduction are both sources of genetic variation. What is the key difference between them?

  • A Only mutations affect the gene pool
  • B Sexual reproduction creates entirely new alleles
  • C Mutation creates new alleles; sexual reproduction reshuffles existing ones
  • D Sexual reproduction is the only source of new variation
✅ C. Mutation is a change in DNA when it is copied or repaired — it creates new alleles for the gene pool. Sexual reproduction (through crossing over, independent assortment, and fertilization) reshuffles existing alleles into new combinations. Both contribute to the genetic variation that natural selection acts on.

The Cell Cell
Q16Multiple Choice

A compound microscope uses two lenses: the ocular lens (10×) and the objective lens (40×). What is the total magnification?

  • A 50×
  • B 400×
  • C 4×
  • D 14×
✅ B — 400×. Total magnification = ocular × objective = 10 × 40 = 400×. The two lenses multiply (not add) their magnifications. This is why microscopes can achieve very high magnifications by combining lenses.
Q17Multiple Choice

Which organelle is known as the "powerhouse of the cell" because it produces ATP through cellular respiration?

  • A Ribosome
  • B Nucleus
  • C Mitochondria
  • D Golgi apparatus
✅ C — Mitochondria. The mitochondria convert glucose and oxygen into ATP (adenosine triphosphate) — the cell's energy currency — through cellular respiration. Cells with high energy demands (like muscle cells) have more mitochondria. The nucleus stores DNA; ribosomes make proteins; the Golgi packages and ships proteins.
Q18Multiple Choice

What is the main difference between a prokaryotic and a eukaryotic cell?

  • A Prokaryotes have no membrane-bound nucleus; eukaryotes do
  • B Eukaryotes have no cell membrane; prokaryotes do
  • C Prokaryotes have more organelles than eukaryotes
  • D Eukaryotes are always smaller than prokaryotes
✅ A. The defining difference is the nucleus. Prokaryotes (like bacteria) have no membrane-bound nucleus — their DNA floats freely in the cytoplasm. Eukaryotes (like plant, animal, and fungal cells) have a true nucleus enclosed by a membrane. Eukaryotes also generally have more organelles.
Q19Multiple Choice

The movement of water from an area of high water concentration to low water concentration across a semi-permeable membrane is called:

  • A Active transport
  • B Facilitated diffusion
  • C Endocytosis
  • D Osmosis
✅ D — Osmosis. Osmosis is the passive movement of water across a semi-permeable membrane from high to low water concentration (or equivalently, from low to high solute concentration). It requires no energy. Active transport moves substances against their gradient and requires ATP.

Diversity of Life Diversity
Q20Multiple Choice

Bacteria are classified as prokaryotes. Which of the following is a unique feature of bacterial cells?

  • A They have a membrane-bound nucleus
  • B They have a cell wall made of peptidoglycan
  • C They contain chloroplasts
  • D They reproduce only sexually
✅ B — Peptidoglycan cell wall. Bacterial cell walls are made of peptidoglycan, a unique polymer not found in eukaryotes. This is why antibiotics like penicillin can target bacteria without harming human cells — they attack peptidoglycan synthesis. Bacteria reproduce asexually through binary fission.
Q21Multiple Choice

Protists are best described as:

  • A Prokaryotic organisms with no nucleus
  • B Multicellular organisms only
  • C Mostly unicellular eukaryotes that don't fit into other kingdoms
  • D Fungi that live in water
✅ C. Protists are a diverse group of mostly unicellular eukaryotes that are essentially defined by what they are not — not animals, plants, or fungi. They include amoeba, paramecia, algae, and plasmodium (the malaria parasite). Some are photosynthetic, some are heterotrophic.
Q22Multiple Choice

Which characteristic distinguishes fungi from plants?

  • A Fungi are heterotrophs — they cannot make their own food
  • B Fungi have chloroplasts
  • C Fungi have no cell wall
  • D Fungi are prokaryotes
✅ A — Fungi are heterotrophs. Unlike plants which photosynthesize (autotrophs), fungi cannot make their own food. They are decomposers — they secrete enzymes to break down organic matter externally and absorb the nutrients. Fungi do have cell walls, but made of chitin (not cellulose like plants), and they are eukaryotes.
Q23Multiple Choice

Which of the following animal groups is characterized by having a backbone (vertebral column)?

  • A Insects
  • B Mollusks
  • C Arthropods
  • D Vertebrates
✅ D — Vertebrates. Vertebrates (fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals) are defined by their vertebral column (backbone). Insects, mollusks, and arthropods are all invertebrates — they make up the vast majority of animal species on Earth.
Q24Multiple Choice

What structure in plant cells allows them to perform photosynthesis?

  • A Mitochondria
  • B Chloroplast
  • C Cell wall
  • D Vacuole
✅ B — Chloroplast. Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll, the green pigment that absorbs sunlight. They convert light energy, CO₂, and water into glucose and oxygen through photosynthesis. Animal cells have no chloroplasts — this is a key feature that distinguishes plant and animal cells.

Ecology Ecology
Q25Multiple Choice

The nonliving (physical and chemical) factors of an environment are called:

  • A Biotic factors
  • B Ecosystems
  • C Abiotic factors
  • D Populations
✅ C — Abiotic factors. Abiotic (non-living) factors include temperature, water, sunlight, soil, and pH. Biotic factors are the living components (organisms, their interactions). Together, biotic and abiotic factors make up an ecosystem — every living thing is shaped by both.
Q26Multiple Choice

In a food chain, which organisms are the primary producers?

  • A Plants and photosynthetic organisms
  • B Herbivores
  • C Carnivores
  • D Decomposers
✅ A — Plants and photosynthetic organisms. Producers (autotrophs) form the base of every food chain — they capture solar energy and convert it into chemical energy (glucose). All other organisms ultimately depend on producers. Herbivores are primary consumers; carnivores are secondary or tertiary consumers; decomposers break down dead matter.
Q27Multiple Choice

A relationship where one organism benefits and the other is neither harmed nor helped is called:

  • A Mutualism
  • B Parasitism
  • C Predation
  • D Commensalism
✅ D — Commensalism. In commensalism, one organism benefits while the other is unaffected (e.g. barnacles on a whale). Mutualism = both benefit (e.g. bees and flowers). Parasitism = one benefits, one is harmed (e.g. tapeworm in a host). Predation = one organism eats another.
